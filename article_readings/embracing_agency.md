# Embracing Agency: How We Direct Our Lives with Intentionality and Reflection

## What does it mean to be an "agent" of your own life?
An agent is someone who takes action on behalf of others. A human agent is someone who takes action on behalf of themselves - deliberately, thoughtfully, and purposefully. The human capability to influence one's own functioning and the course of events through one's actions - doing them intentionally

## The four core features
### Intentionality: acting with purpose
People form intentions that include action plans and strategies for realizing them. 
### Forethrough: living ahead of the present moment
People set goals for themselves and foresee likely outcomes of prospective actions to guide and motivate their efforts anticipatorily
### Self-reactiveness: regulating yourself in real time
Agents adopt personal standards and monitor and regulate their actions by self-reactive influence - doing things that give them satisfaction and a sense of self-worth, and refraining from actions that bring self-censure.
**self-censure is the conscious or unconscious act of restricting one's own expression, behavior, or emotions to align with perceived social expectations or norms**
### Self-reflectiveness: examining your own functioning
Through functional self-awareness, people reflect on their personal efficacy, the soundness of their thoughts and actions, the meaning of their pursuits, and make corrective adjustments if necessary.

## Three modes of agency: individual, proxy, and collective

- Individual agency: a person brings their own influence to bear on what they can control directly.

- Proxy agency: they rely on others who have the resources, knowledge, or access to act on their behalf (patient asking doctor for guidance)

- Collective agency: people pool their knowledge, skills, and resources and act together to shape their shared future
