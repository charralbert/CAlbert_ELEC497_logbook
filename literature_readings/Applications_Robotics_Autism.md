# Applications of Robotics for Autism Spectrum Disorder
link: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s40489-023-00402-5

## Abstract
- Improvement of social skills of children with ASD
- Significant impact of robot therapy in 7/10 studies

## Intro
- Conventional therapies aim to increase social engagement, interaction, and communication
- Social assistive robots (SARs)
- "Robots have the advantage of being very repetitive and stylised in their appearance, turning out to be more predictable and emotionally more comfortable for ASD children. Moreover, they can smoothly execute one task at a time, making the learning process more focused and simpler." They are object which can occupy a physical space, unlike other therapies like computer games and VR - thus can be applied to everyday life

## Methods

**Five different types of control**
1. Wizard of Oz: the robot is fully remotely controlled by a hidden operator
2. Hybrid control: the robot has some autonomous behaviors, but the overall control is done through an interface, used by an adult or child
3. Semi-autonomous control: the robot behaves autonomously, but all the decisions require the approval of the supervising adult
4. Fully autonomous strategy: the robot behaves autonomously with no intervention by any operator
5. Autonomous and adaptive strategy: the robot perceives the reactions of the subjects and adapts its behaviors according to them

### Robots
75% of the considered studies used robots due to human resemblance and capability to perform different tasks - NAO being the most used.
