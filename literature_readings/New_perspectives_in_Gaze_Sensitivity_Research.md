# New Perspectives in Gaze Sensitivity Research
link: https://link.springer.com/article/10.3758/s13420-015-0204-z?

## Intro
- Gaze following (co-orienting gaze) and gaze aversion, animals repond fearfully to direct gaze because forward facing eyes are associated with predator attacks.

## Attention Attribution and Visual Perspective Taking
"In gaze sensitivity context, attention attribution refers to the ability to infer where another individual's attention is directed based on where they are looking"

"When given the opportunity to direct begging gestures towards one of two human experimenters. Only after numerous trials did chimpanzees gradually learn to beg from the individual who could see them. In a similar study, apes did not require learning to beg towards attentive humans, but they did so only if both body and face were directed towards them, and the subjects did not distinguish betwen open and closed eyes. In contrast, both dogs and horses preferentially approached attentive humans in order to obtain food."

## Gaze Sensitivity and Learning
"Chimpanzees and ravens, but not macaques, will stop responding to a human repeatedly orienting their head and eye gaze towards a location with nothing of interest through habituation learning."

"Defensive behavior in response to predators may change depending on the frequency, duration, or type of experience. Habituation or close proximity with humans may greatly influence whether prey are ever close enough to a predator to perceive differences in gaze orientation or to associate different gaze cues with specific intentions or actions"
