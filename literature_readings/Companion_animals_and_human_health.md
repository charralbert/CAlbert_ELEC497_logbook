# Companion Animals and Human Health: Benefits, Challenges, and the Road Ahead
link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S155878781000016X

## Abstract
Human-animal interactions can remarkably enhance human physical health and psychological well-being. 

## Intro
- Human relationships with companion animals are not new.
- Wild and tamed animals as companions.
- Spend money on pets for nothing in return

## Initial Research
- coronary heart disease - pet owners were more likely to be alive than nonowners. Coronary heart disease is a stress-related disease, protective effect of pet ownership is due to its effect on psychologicala risk factors. Thus, by reducing stress and improving mental health, companion animals may make their owners more likely to survive. 
- dog and cat owner had better mental health than nonowners - fewer doctors visits and were less likely to be on medication for heart problems or sleeping difficulties. 

## Biophilia Hypothesis
**Human beings have an innate propensity to attend to and be attracted by other animals and living things.**
- evolutionarily: for increasing chances of survival
- today: calming, relaxing, pleasant. 
- viewing animals, regardless of whether they are familial or bonded, can reduce anxiety and tension.

## Social Support Hypothesis
**Companion Animals are a social support in and of themselves and also that they act as facilitators of social interactions between other human beings**
- constant availability, nonjugmental support, and unconditional love. As such, people form strong attachments to their pets.
- Cancer patients: presence of companion animal lessens their fears, despair, loneliness, and isolation and enables them to adapt to their extremely difficult situations.
- Crucial for elderly, often lose human social support because of friends and family moving away or passing away.
- Companion animals act as social lubricants, they facilitate social interactions for their owners because they encourage appraoches by others and stimulate conversation.
- Presence of an animal may help people overcome social isolation because of the socializing effects of animals.

## Application
- Increased survival rates, decreased medication use, and fewer visits to physicians. Biophilia hypothesis highlights the capacity of human-animal interactions to reduce cardiovascular, behavioral, and psychological indicators of stress and anxiety. The social support theory emphasizes the capacity of human-animal interactions to reduce loneliness and increase psychological well being and life satisfaction. 
- numerous benefits have been reported from animal-assisted interventions. Examples include reduced anxiety during and after therapeutic sessions; improved rapport and communication between patients and therapists, enhanced attendance.
- kids with ADHDs - animal assisted intervention program. Results showed that participants in the animal-assisted intervention condition showed increased attendance, cooperation, and engagement in learning, and decreased antisocial and violent behavior.
- Animal assisted interventions for children with Autism. Children with ASD tend to be rejected and victimized by peers which can lead to social isolation, loneliness, and depression. Study examined the effect of therapy with a dog, a stuffed dog, and a ball. In the presence of the dog, children with ASD demonstrated increased playfulness, focus, and social awareness. 

## Challenges
- renting apartments/homes can mean no animals.
- restricted from community spaces: hospitals, nursing homes, educational institutions.
