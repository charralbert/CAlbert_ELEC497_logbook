# Human-animal interactions in livestock production
link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0168159102002800

## Abstract
- interactions between stockpeople and their animals can limit the productivity of animals

## Reseach on human-animal interactions in the livestock industries
- defined attitude as "a psychological tendency that is expressed by evaluating a particular entity with some degree of favor or disfavor" and psychologists generally define three components to attitude: cognition, the person's beliefs about the object; affect, the emotional response to the object, and conation, the behavioural tendency towards the object.
- can infer attitude based on components

- approach-avoidance responses develop as a consequence aversive and rewarding elements of bouts. Aversive: slaps, shouting, and fast speed of movement, positive: pats, strokes, hand of the stockperson resting on the animal, talking, and slow deliberate movement. Some positive interactions may just be neutral, but through frequent use leads to reduced avoidance of humans by animals.
