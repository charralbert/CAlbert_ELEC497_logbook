## Maslow's Hierarchy
1. Physiological Needs
- food, water, sleep, shelter
2. Safety Needs
- Security, stability, protection from danger
3. Love and Belongingness
- Relationships, intimacy, group belonging
4. Esteem Needs
- self-respect, competence, recognition
5. Self-Actualization
- Achieving one's full potential, creativity, personal growth

"If a nonhuman animal has insecurities around basic needs, it may be difficult to consider forming or maintaining more complex social relationships. If basic needs are met, however, then additional physical and psychological factors can be considered and used to build the foundation of relationships, both within and between species"

## Relationship Stages
"All stages of relationships are defined by different levels of trust and interactions. Trust can be described as "willingness of an entity" to become vulnerable to another entity. In taking this risk, the trustor presumes the trustee will act in a way that is conducive to the trustor's welfare despite the trustee's actions being outside the trustor's control."

### Stage 1: Physical Trust
"All animals must assess physical threat prior to engaging in trust at higher levels. In trust research in humans this level can be called strategic trust or calculus-based trust, which is where basic physical safety is established and indidivudals recognize that the other party is not a threat. This stage also includes a level of fragility where trust is subject to changes based on continued interactions, exchanges, and experiences and basic concepts of trust within interactions that align with threat assessment. For animals, this stage is established when either the fear response is evident via avoidant or flight behaviors, for nonhuman animals, this stage is established when either the fear response is evident or the animal is willing to engage of their own volition. Generally, an individual who does not feel safe and has not yet established their level of trust will either cease to move towards or move away. 

**Associated Interactions with Physical Safety**
"Physical proximity aligns with physical safety, it is important to allow freedome of movement to provide inidividuals with the ability to choose the level of space needed to feel physically safe" "..Assessing behavioral responses at different proximities and adjusting space to allow for increased feeling of safety. Ideally, enough time and space are given to allow for the development of subjective feelings of physical safety, at which time additional elements, such as novel objects or food, can be added to encourage seeking behaviors."

**Associated Interactions with Building Mutual Language and Communication**
Language and Communication are built through the consistency of interactions with predictable outcomes. Through the establishment of consistent interactions and corresponding consistent responses, each individual can better assess the intentions of the other based on approach, reatreat, and other behaviors that indicate intention, motivation, or emotion. 

**Additional Considerations for Physical Safety and Building Mutual Language and Connection**
"The closer the individual, the higher the potential threat. How an individual appraoches and maintains proximity can provide additional information as to the subjective experience of safety of the individual, with fear responses often resulting in behaviors that decrease proximity. This is often referred to as the flight zone."
"Communication and shared language are being developed at this stage, so it is critical to be as consistent as possible when building a shared language. Predictability is also constantly assessed since the relationship is still based on limited interactions. In this case, safety is closely tied to predictability meaning that inconsisten communication reduces predictability and may consequently be perceived as less safe. If physical interactions occur at this stage, great care should be taken to maintain consistency to achieve mutual understanding."

### Stage 2: Psychological Safety, Ongoing Exchanges, and Rupture and Reconciliation
"Once physical safety has been established, individuals build trust through exchanges and repeated transactions where predictability, reliability, and, in the case of humans, empathy" 
"Communication.... focuses on expressing subjective feelings of physical safety and exists mostly to convey information regarding seeking (curiousity and exploration) or fear (moving away)"
"If physical safety has been established through repeated interactions, additional communication may be developed that aligns with other emotions such as play, lust, grief, or even caregiving."

### Stage 3: Deeper Relationship and Friendship
Under these circumstances, humans and animals will associate their friends with physical and psychological safety if other uncertainties are present. This means they will gravitate towards their friends under stressful conditions to find comfort in social interactions.
