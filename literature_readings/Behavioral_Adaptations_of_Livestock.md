# Behavioral adaptations of livestock to environmental stressors: implications for welfaare and productivity
link: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/09712119.2025.2583108#abstract

## Intro
**Environmental Stressors**
External elements that disrupt livestock's physiological, reproductive, and productive balance can be physical (heat, cold, etc..), biological (parasites), chemical (pollutants). The manifestation of stress - panting or shade-seeking, then disruptions in thermal regulation, then altered growth, reproduction, or immune responses. 

## Mechanism of Livestock Adaptation
**Physiological responses**
- increased respiration, heart rate, vasolidation (increased blood flow), and sweating.

## Behavioral Indicators of Stress and Adaptation
- Panting: rapid, shallow breathing response observed in many livestock species.
- Shivering
- Changes in posture
- Pacing
