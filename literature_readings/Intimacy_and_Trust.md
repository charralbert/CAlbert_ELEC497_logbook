# Intimacy and Trust in Interpersonal Relationships: A Sociological Perspective
link: https://www.clausiuspress.com/assets/default/article/2024/05/19/article_1716108953.pdf

## Abstract
- Intimacy: emotional closeness and support, has been shown to enhance the stability and quality of relationships, while trust serves as a fundamental element in maintaining and strengthening interpersonal bonds. 
- trust promotes the maintenance and development of intimacy in relationships

## Concept and Function of Intimacy
- Sense of emotional closeness, understanding, and mutual support, characterized by a deep level of trust, vulnerability, and affection within relationships. Encompasses romantic relationships, friendships, familial bonds...
- Impact: fostering relationship satisfaction, stability, and well-being. Intimate partners are more likely to feel understood, valued, and validated.
- intimacy stands as a cornerstone of healty and fulfilling interpersonal relationships, encompassing a rich tapestry of emotional closeness, support, and connection. 

## Concept and Significance of Trust
- Trust: individual's positive evaluation of others and the belief that these others will fulfill their obligations when it matters. The elements of trust include the trustor's willingness to trust others, positive inferences about the trustee, and the trustor's ability to tolerate uncertainty.
- Trust is a key factor in promoting relationship stability and quality. In trusting relationships, individuals are more inclined to share their thoughts, feelings, and experiences while also being more receptive to others' perspectives and emotional feedback. Trust promotes effective communication, coordination, and cooperation, reducing conflict and misunderstanding. 

## Interplay between Intimacy and Trust
- Positive correlation between intimacy and trust. Intimacy and trust tend to co-evolve over time, with increases in one variable leading to corresponding increases in the other. 
- Intimacy and trust are integral components of successful interpersonal relationships, with intimacy facilitating trust building and trust enhancing the development and maintenance of intimacy. 

## Factors Influencing Intimacy and Trust
**Relationship Factors**
Consistency in attitudes, both parties share similar core values and goals - more likely to establish resonance and close connection. Communication style: positive, honest, respectful, supportive.
**Environmental Factors**
different values, behavior patterns, and methods of expression. Different cultures = different views and expectations.
