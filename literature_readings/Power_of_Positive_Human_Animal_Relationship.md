## Abstract
- paying attention to th animal's behavioral response to humans and providing choice and control to the animal in terms of when and how to interact with humans. 

## Intro
- Domestic animals often seek and enjoy interacting with humans, beyond depending on humans for food. Animals may perceive interacting with humans per se as rewarding

## Mechanisms for the Formation of a Positive HAR
- positive HAR can be defined operationally in that the animals shows voluntary approach and spatial proximity and sings of anticipation, pleasure, relaxation, or other indicators of a rewarding experience arising from interacting with the human.

## Interactions versus Relationship
- The formation of a relationship is a progressive process, reinforced upon subsequent interactions. This highlights the difference between an interaction and a relationship based on a single v multiple events between two individuals, with a relationship developing on the basis that animals are able to memorize and predict future interactions with humans. 

**Associative Learning**
Associative learning can accelerate the formation of a relationshup by the animal associating humans with positive aspects either through classical conditioning or operant conditioning. A positive HAR can be established by human contact that is inherently rewarding (stroking/brushing)
- food can also facilitate

**Bonding**
- familiarity with a human does not necessarily equate to a positive HAR, but may be conducive. 

## Assessment of a Positive Human-Animal Relationship
**Behavioral Changes**
Chronological order the behavioral changes associated with a positive HAR:
1. animal shows signs of anticipation before interaction in cases when the HI is predictable. Pacing, vocalizations 
2. Orientation response - turns attention toward the human. Head, ear, body posture to distinguish between positive and negative
3. Latency to approach - voluntary seeking behavior of the animal. Lack of approach does not preclude a positive HAR but may just indicate low motivation.
4. Number or duration of interactions initiated by the animal is often used as an indicator of the quality of the HAR.

Animals may also expose body areas where they wish to be stroked. These types of behavioral reponses, exposing often vulnerable body region, may be interpreted as involving a level of trust reflecting positive HAR. 

Lack of avoidance is indicative of low fear of humans. Ear positioning (forward v side/backward). Reluctance to interacat or conflicting motivations - negative indicators

## Epistemoiological Considerations for the Investigation of a Positive Human-Animal Relationship
An animal voluntarily approaching and interacting (non-aggressively) with a human is a prime indicator of a positive HAR. Not sufficient to qualify as a specific positive HAR because the animal may approach and interact because of curiosity. 

Avoidance responses (distance of withdrawal by the animal from an approaching human) are used to mention the fear dimension of the HAR. 
Situations where there is a lack of control offered to the animal because the animal is restrained or limited to a constrained space, etc... may be important that the animal is provided with a sense of control or agency by free-choice approach about when and how to interact. 

## Implications for Practice 

### Developing and Cultivating a Positive Human-Animal Relationship
Dynamic and reciprocal process modulated by individual and contextual features. 
- Gentle handling.
- some species may not need physical contact
- brief opportunities to interact with humans over days - weeks are sufficient to reduce fear.
- positive reinforcement
- may be easier with younger animals because less negative experiences, greater learning ability, and higher levels of curiosity and exploration than adult animals. 
- previous positive experience can help generalize to all.
