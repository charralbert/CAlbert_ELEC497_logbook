## 📝 Discussion Summary

To be able to review code setup, organization, and practices, Professor Pan gave me access to the Queen's MItHRIL GitLab. 

Then, we discussed the project plan: I will develop a robotic character with the ur5e robotic arm. The guiding question for this project is: "How do we develop trust in Human-Robot interaction?" and "How can we make the robot feel like a character, and not a robot?" 

I will be in charge of Turkish Ice Cream (TIC) robot demonstrations and will employ the help of a lab member to teach me how to run it. 

Additionally, to familiarize myself with the ur5e, I will study the code in the GitLab and execute the code for the study described in "The Social Life of Robot Arms" written by former lab member, Roy El-Helou. 
To classify and describe motion, I will research laban movement. Professor Pan introduced the framework of using a visual language action model to llm to actions, researching these methods and subsequent pipeline will aid my efforts.

On Tuesday, 05-19, we will take a trip to discuss research project with connected minds.

The final report for this project is due August 4th as described by Queen's University ELEC 497 course structure. Thus, through now to the beginning of June, the main focus will be platform development. Following that, a user study should be conducted and results during end of June to beginning of July. End of July to beginning of August will be writing the report for submission.

Due to the nature of conducting a study, we will need to submit a request to the general research ethics board through Queen's University.
